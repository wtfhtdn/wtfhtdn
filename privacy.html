Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

What we collect

Very little. When you visit this site, we count the number of page loads to understand roughly how many people are using it. We do not collect your IP address, location, browser details, or any personally identifying information. We do not use cookies. We do not use third-party analytics.

If you click a share button, we record that a share occurred and which platform it was on. We do not record what was shared or who shared it.

If you submit a suggestion via the suggestion box, the text you enter is stored anonymously. No name, email address, or identifying information is attached to it.

What we don't collect

We don't track you across sessions. We don't know who you are. We don't use advertising networks. We don't sell data because we don't have any worth selling.

Third parties

News content is sourced from publicly available RSS feeds published by BBC, Guardian, Al Jazeera, Sky News, NYT, Financial Times, ProPublica, DW News, and others. Summaries are generated using the Anthropic API. If you click through to a story, you leave this site and that outlet's own privacy policy applies.

If you donate via Liberapay, their privacy policy applies to that transaction.

If you share a story via X, Bluesky, or Threads, that platform's privacy policy applies.

The site is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub's privacy statement applies to the hosting layer.
